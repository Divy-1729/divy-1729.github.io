My favorite music to listen to

I spend a lot of time listening to music. Over the years I have curated a few go-to albums and artists that fit different parts of my day. Below are the kinds of music I reach for while studying, coding, grading, commuting, or working out. I have grouped the recommendations by activity to make it easy to pick something that suits the moment.

Studying

When I am at the University of Toronto deep in problem sets, instrumental soundtracks help me focus. The Oppenheimer soundtrack is a favorite. Tracks like "American Prometheus" stand out for their intensity and atmosphere. I also enjoy Hans Zimmer's work, especially the Dune soundtrack, which creates a wide, ethereal space that keeps me motivated while I race through assignments or wrestle with a tricky concept, like using integrating factors to solve differential equations.

Coding

Coding sessions, especially debugging PyTorch or wrestling with dependency issues, call for something calm and steady. I often put on mellow, psychedelic folk to stay centered. Favorites I return to are "Another One" by Mac DeMarco and "So Tonight I Might See" by Mazzy Star. These albums help me slow down, breathe, and get back into a focused mindset when the code is fighting back.

Grading and Other Mundane Tasks

When I am grading, I want to stay sharp and move efficiently. Fast-paced, attention-grabbing music helps me keep momentum. Arctic Monkeys' album AM is a reliable pick because its length often matches a single grading session. I also enjoy Fleetwood Mac, especially live recordings like "The Dance" for variety and energy.

Commute

I walk and take transit a lot, so music that sets the pace for my steps or eases a long ride is essential. On walks, Radiohead's "In Rainbows" is great for keeping a steady rhythm. For bus rides, I often play Alice in Chains' "Dirt" or other classic grunge like Nirvana and Pearl Jam. The rawness of grunge turns long or bumpy rides into something cathartic.

For downtown commutes on the GO train, I sometimes listen to the Succession soundtrack. The theme's beat gives a satisfying sense of arrival when I step off at Union Station. On long flights home, I return to Radiohead, especially earlier albums like "The Bends" and "OK Computer", and also enjoy Slowdive and Pink Floyd for their expansive soundscapes.

Gym

At the gym I prefer high-energy music. Bands and artists I often play include Rammstein, Deftones, Linkin Park, and Creed. These acts provide the drive I need for heavy sets and intense cardio.

Parting Thoughts

These picks represent what I reach for most days. I plan to write more posts exploring specific albums, playlists, and the stories behind why certain music works for different tasks. For now, I hope something on this list helps you find a soundtrack for your next study session, coding sprint, commute, or workout.

Until next time — take care.
